The Hidden Map of Funding.

Higher education and professional growth shouldn't be gated by your bank account. For DEI communities, specialized aid exists to bridge the systemic gaps.

1. Federal & State Foundations

The Pell Grant

Need-based aid that doesn't need to be repaid. Essential for students from low-income backgrounds.

FAFSA for First-Gen

The gateway to almost all aid. Pro tip: Complete it as early as possible (October 1st) to maximize your chances.

2. DEI-Specific Scholarships

UNCF & HSF

The United Negro College Fund and Hispanic Scholarship Fund provide billions in support for Black and Latino students.

Point Foundation

The nation's largest scholarship-granting organization for LGBTQ+ students of merit.

3. Professional Development Aid

Aid isn't just for college. Look for "Returnship" grants, coding bootcamp scholarships for underrepresented groups, and professional association stipends.

Action Step

Search for "Professional Association for [Your Identity] in [Your Field]"—most have hidden grant programs.
